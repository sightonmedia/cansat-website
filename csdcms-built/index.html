The Mission

A real science instrument.
A real flight.

A CanSat is a pseudo-satellite small enough to fit inside a soft-drink can — roughly 66 mm diameter, 115 mm tall — that carries sensors, collects flight data and descends safely under a parachute the team designs and builds. It is not a simulation and it is not a model: it goes up, it comes down, and the science it records is real.

Choose your path

Two ways to fly

Both categories share the same core science mission. How far you go — and how you get your CanSat into the sky — is up to your team.

Beginner

Drone Drop

We supply a free CanSat kit. Your team assembles it, programs the sensors, and we release it from a drone at roughly 120 metres — a real descent under a real parachute your team designs.

Learn more →
Advanced

Rocket Launch

Your team travels to Lethbridge, Alberta, and your CanSat is launched by sounding rocket to above 1 km — transmitting live telemetry all the way down. The top team represents Canada at the ESA CanSat conference in the Netherlands.

Why it matters
"The best way to learn aerospace engineering is to actually do aerospace engineering."

CSDCMS is a registered Canadian charity whose entire purpose is to give secondary-school students a credible, structured path into the space industry — one solder joint, one sensor calibration, and one launch campaign at a time.
